Why this needs you

🧠 I have the technical pieces — but not the right problem yet

I've set up the tools (audio analysis libraries, ML stack, prototyping environment). What I don't have is a clear sense of what would actually be useful — and that's where you come in.

If you're a therapist, a patient, a musician, a student, a researcher, or just someone with a hunch — I want to hear from you.

Seeds I'm exploring (your ideas might be better)

🔍 Music analysis that explains itself

Extract tempo, key, energy, emotional color from any song — and show why the analysis says what it says. A technical foundation for everything else.

Ask yourself: If you could see the inner structure of any piece of music, what would you do with it?

🎯 Find or create the perfect sound

Search by feeling, not by genre. "Something calm but not sleepy, for 20 minutes of focused work" — and the system finds or generates it.

Ask yourself: Have you ever needed a very specific kind of music and couldn't find it? What was the situation?

🛠️ A better toolkit for music therapists

Many therapists build playlists and analyze music by hand. A tool that handles the busywork could free them up to focus on people.

Ask yourself: If you're a practitioner — what part of your workflow is the most tedious? If you're a patient — what would make sessions feel more personalized?
Open questions (anything could go)
Q1 Could an AI help people understand why a particular song moves them — not just classify it as "happy" or "sad"?
Q2 What would a useful music therapy tool look like for someone with dementia, autism, or PTSD — not a general "relaxation" app, but something targeted?
Q3 Is the biggest need in music therapy assessment, intervention, or education — and where does computer science fit best?
Q4 What's something you've wished existed — a playlist generator with a slider for "familiar vs novel" or "simple vs complex" — that no one has built?
