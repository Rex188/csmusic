Music × Computer Science
for Therapy

I'm exploring how computer science can help music therapists and the people they work with. This page tracks the ideas — and I'd love your thoughts.

Ideas I'm exploring
Direction 1

Reading the hidden fingerprint of any song

Every piece of music has a structure — tempo, key, energy, emotional color. Can we extract these automatically from audio, accurately and clearly? This is a signal-processing problem at heart, but it's the foundation for everything else.

In plain terms: You upload a song, the computer tells you what makes it tick — fast or slow, major or minor, calm or tense — and shows you why it sounds the way it does.
Direction 2

Finding the right sound for the right moment

If we understand what makes music feel a certain way, we can search for or even generate music that matches a specific need — calm for anxiety, rhythm for movement, familiarity for comfort.

In plain terms: "I need something energetic but not chaotic, for 15 minutes of exercise" — and the system finds or creates it.
Direction 3

Building better tools for music therapists

Music therapists currently do a lot of analysis and playlist building by hand. A purpose-built tool could save them hours and help them match music more precisely to each client's needs.

In plain terms: Give professionals better instruments — not replacing their expertise, but removing the busywork so they can focus on people.
Where things stand
